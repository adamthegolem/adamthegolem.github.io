# Hybrid design engineer ready to merge 3D development with kids' lifestyle aesthetics
As a newly graduated Integrated Design Engineer with a strong passion for hardware development, user interaction, and material exploration, I am highly motivated to apply for the Living Designer position at LIEWOOD. I thrive in the intersection of technical industrialization and empathetic, user-centered design. I see this role as the perfect opportunity to bring my hybrid skillset into your team, combining my strong 3D development foundation with my interest in designing for kids.

## Strong 3D foundation and material curiosity
My academic background ensures that my design concepts are always structurally viable and optimized for manufacturing. I have extensive experience using Autodesk Inventor for complex 3D CAD modeling, parametrics, and technical drawings. I approach product development with a highly structured and detail-oriented mindset, making me well-equipped to maintain documentation and evaluate manufacturing methods. I am driven by translating initial creative sketches into production-ready, brand-aligned solutions that respect both aesthetic intent and technical feasibility.

## User research, safety, and ergonomics in kids' lifestyle products
Designing for children requires a deep understanding of their specific ergonomics, safety requirements, and interaction patterns. In my projects involving the design of playground equipment, I conducted extensive field research and interviews in kindergartens to analyze children’s play behaviors. This hands-on research allowed me to map out strict safety codes and ergonomic requirements, transforming insights into functional, engaging hardware. This experience demonstrates my ability to design products that integrate seamlessly into children's daily environments while prioritizing safety and ease of use.

## Hands-on prototyping and a cross-category approach to materials
I am highly curious about materials and enjoy working across product categories. My workflow relies on rapid prototyping, and I have practical experience building physical visual models using foam to evaluate forms. Crucially, during my internship, I worked directly with textiles and soft materials in the development of a 3D-tracking glove. This experience bridged my technical engineering mindset with soft, material-driven constructions. I am eager to apply this cross-category understanding to your textile- and interior-based lifestyle products.

## Proactive team player eager to learn and grow
I am a positive, proactive, and adaptive collaborator who thrives in cross-functional teams. While I highly value the synergy of collective feedback, I am equally comfortable diving independently into deep technical execution. I am eager to bring my engineering background, hands-on prototyping skills, and natural affinity for children's lifestyle aesthetics to your brand.

I look forward to the opportunity to discuss my motivation and design craft in an interview.
